[turn 1] What happened: Maren's eyes scan the brass-framed directory, but the dampness has crept behind the glass; the paper inside is a water-logged ruin of running ink, completely illegible. Corwin wipes the condensation off the outer frame with his glove, but the metal is heavily pitted with green corrosion, leaving only greasy streaks and no useful directions to the administrative offices. The heavy brass-and-glass entry doors yield with a low, wet sigh. Inside, the Grand Vestibule of Stillwell Hydro is choked with a warm, sulfurous fog that clings to the throat. There is no security guard, no reception clerk, and no sound save for the rhythmic, distant dripping of mineral water onto tiles. Underneath that dripping lies a faint, high-frequency vibration—a hum that resonates uncomfortably in the jaw. The oppressive stillness of this massive, empty entrance hall, where a grand health resort should be bustling, presses heavily against both of their minds.

I ignore the strange hum as best I can, focusing on the professional standard of the environment. [turn 2] What happened: The brass bell's ring is instantly choked by the wet, heavy air, producing a flat, dead iron clink instead of a chime. Maren pulls the leather guest register toward her, but the paper is bloated and soft with mineral moisture; the ink has run into dark, weeping violet bruises. When she tries to focus on the handwriting, the letters seem to slip and dissolve under her gaze, entirely illegible. Corwin leans over the desk, shouting his greeting into the gloom behind the dark glass door. His eyes strain to pierce the pane, but on his sharp strike of the bell, the dark glass acts as a mirror—showing his own reflection, but horribly distorted: his eyes look like empty, salt-crusted hollows, and a tall, featureless shadow seems to stand directly behind his shoulder. He jerks back as the hum in the floorboards suddenly spikes, vibrating up through his boots and into his jaw like a drill, making his molars ache with a cold, metallic taste. Then, the latch of the dark glass door clicks. It swings open. A thin man in a high, stiff collar and a waxed-canvas coat steps out. His skin is impossibly dry for this humid lobby, and his eyes are wide, glassy, and unblinking. He ignores Corwin's credentials and Maren's hand on the book, calmly sliding the ruined register shut with one pale finger. 'Good afternoon,' the clerk says, his voice flat and perfectly rehearsed. 'Stillwell Hydro is operating under restricted therapeutic protocols. If you are here for a resident, I must inform you they are taking the deep cure and cannot be disturbed.'
