# Character A's private memory

Only what this character has personally witnessed or been told.

[turn 1] I: I adjust my grip on my briefcase and pull my coat tighter against the sulfurous damp, eyes scanning the brass-framed directory before approaching the main entrance. I make a deliberate show of checking my pocket watch against the building's silence, then approach the entry doors, searching for any sign of a security check-in or a reception desk within. (The ride is over and the transport has left, confirming I'm committed to this site. The sensory details of the facility—the mineral smells, the rhythmic pump—match the industrial profile of Stillwell Hydro, but the silence is unnerving. I need to establish my persona as a regulatory auditor immediately to gain access. I'll use the directory to orient myself toward the administrative wing, hoping to find a contact who handles personnel records so I can cross-reference Tomas's whereabouts without raising suspicion about my personal interest.)
[turn 1] What happened: Maren's eyes scan the brass-framed directory, but the dampness has crept behind the glass; the paper inside is a water-logged ruin of running ink, completely illegible. Corwin wipes the condensation off the outer frame with his glove, but the metal is heavily pitted with green corrosion, leaving only greasy streaks and no useful directions to the administrative offices. The heavy brass-and-glass entry doors yield with a low, wet sigh. Inside, the Grand Vestibule of Stillwell Hydro is choked with a warm, sulfurous fog that clings to the throat. There is no security guard, no reception clerk, and no sound save for the rhythmic, distant dripping of mineral water onto tiles. Underneath that dripping lies a faint, high-frequency vibration—a hum that resonates uncomfortably in the jaw. The oppressive stillness of this massive, empty entrance hall, where a grand health resort should be bustling, presses heavily against both of their minds.

[turn 2] I: I approach the heavy mahogany reception desk, keeping my movements precise and deliberate. I ignore the strange hum as best I can, focusing on the professional standard of the environment. I reach out and press the brass service bell once, firmly, then turn my attention to the leather guest register, flipping open to the most recent entries to see if any names look familiar—or if there's any sign of maintenance logs that might explain the 'terraces' mention. (The directory outside was useless, so I must rely on internal documentation. The guest register is the most logical place to start to see who has been through here recently. If I can find a maintenance log or a register of personnel, I might find another lead on Tomas. I need to maintain the auditor persona, so ringing the bell is the standard, polite procedure, even if the building seems abandoned; it establishes my role as someone who belongs here and expects service.)
[turn 2] What happened: The brass bell's ring is instantly choked by the wet, heavy air, producing a flat, dead iron clink instead of a chime. Maren pulls the leather guest register toward her, but the paper is bloated and soft with mineral moisture; the ink has run into dark, weeping violet bruises. When she tries to focus on the handwriting, the letters seem to slip and dissolve under her gaze, entirely illegible. Corwin leans over the desk, shouting his greeting into the gloom behind the dark glass door. His eyes strain to pierce the pane, but on his sharp strike of the bell, the dark glass acts as a mirror—showing his own reflection, but horribly distorted: his eyes look like empty, salt-crusted hollows, and a tall, featureless shadow seems to stand directly behind his shoulder. He jerks back as the hum in the floorboards suddenly spikes, vibrating up through his boots and into his jaw like a drill, making his molars ache with a cold, metallic taste. Then, the latch of the dark glass door clicks. It swings open. A thin man in a high, stiff collar and a waxed-canvas coat steps out. His skin is impossibly dry for this humid lobby, and his eyes are wide, glassy, and unblinking. He ignores Corwin's credentials and Maren's hand on the book, calmly sliding the ruined register shut with one pale finger. 'Good afternoon,' the clerk says, his voice flat and perfectly rehearsed. 'Stillwell Hydro is operating under restricted therapeutic protocols. If you are here for a resident, I must inform you they are taking the deep cure and cannot be disturbed.'

[turn 3] I: I straighten my posture, offering the clerk a thin, composed smile that doesn't reach my eyes. 'Restricted therapeutic protocols are noted,' I reply, my voice steady and professional. I slide my leather-bound logbook onto the counter, purposefully resting it over the spot where the register was closed. 'However, my authorization for a compliance audit of the Stillwell facility remains absolute regardless of internal guest status. I am here to verify facility safety, not to disturb residents. I will require immediate access to the administrative and personnel logs to confirm the facility’s current staffing capacity and maintenance oversight. Shall we proceed to the registry, or are you suggesting that Stillwell Hydro is currently in violation of the mandated regulatory transparency laws?' (The clerk is an obstacle. His unnatural dryness and complete disregard for our official presence suggests he is either hiding something egregious or he is not entirely human. My goal is to get into that administration office; if I can force him to acknowledge the 'law' or 'audit' status, I might be able to leverage his training against him. I need to keep the pressure on. The fact that the register was ruined and he shut it so quickly suggests they are scrubbing records. I have to find a way to get past him or get him to slip up regarding the personnel files where Tomas would be listed.)
[turn 3] What happened: Maren's formal demands slide off the clerk like water off waxed canvas. He does not so much as blink at her leather-bound logbook, his gaze remaining fixed on the empty air slightly above her shoulder. 'All administrative and personnel logs are currently undergoing active sanitization,' he intones, his voice a flat, dead drone. 'The records are sealed until the cycle completes.' However, when Corwin steps in with his easy merchant's smile and inquiries about bulk transfers, the clerk's neck hinges sideways with a dry, clicking sound. 'Refined stock,' the clerk repeats. 'The bath-salts are drawn and stored below. The storage foreman, Mr. Vance, manages the lower cisterns. He has not returned to his post.' Slowly, the clerk reaches beneath the polished brass counter and produces a heavy, cold brass key, sliding it across the wood toward Corwin. 'You may seek him in the service corridors. Return the key when your business is concluded.' The heavy iron gate leading down to the Lower Corridor stands unlocked, though the dark, humid administrative office remains locked and silent beside them.

[turn 4] I: I maintain my rigid, professional posture, refusing to acknowledge the invitation to the lower cisterns for the moment. Instead, I direct my attention back to the locked Administration Office door, keeping my body angled so the Clerk can see I have no intention of leaving without compliance. 'The storage foreman is not my priority, and I do not wander off-protocol,' I state clearly, my voice cold. I reach into my satchel, withdrawing my department-issue multi-tool. I step toward the administrative door, holding the tool up with the air of an inspector preparing for a necessary bypass of a 'non-compliant' lock. 'Since you claim the logs are undergoing sanitization, it falls to me to verify the status of that process personally. Unless you have the key for this office, I am proceeding with an emergency manual inspection of the records room.' (The Clerk is a dead end—or worse, an imitation. If he's stalling, it's because the administrative files contain the proof I need regarding the transfer records of Tomas. The 'lower cisterns' sound like a distraction or a trap. I have to stay focused on the paper trail. By presenting my tool as an official instrument of a 'manual inspection,' I am trying to maintain the facade of a bureaucrat while putting direct pressure on the office door. If I can get into the administrative files, I might find the real truth before the facility's 'sanitization' deletes it entirely.)
[turn 4] What happened: Maren ignores the clerk's warning, stepping up to the locked Administration Office. With a smooth, practiced twist of her multi-tool, she bypasses the lock with a satisfying click and slips inside before the Clerk can interpose his stiff, waxed-canvas form. Inside, she quickly sorts through the unrefined cabinets. She uncovers a crucial personnel file: Tomas Solvig is marked as 'transferred to Northgate Processing' eight months ago, yet the physical daily clock-in logs prove he was still working here three weeks after his official transfer date. Meanwhile, Corwin slips his brass key into the gate. Blind to the rhythmic, breath-like vibration shaking the floorboards, he assumes it is merely old plumbing. As he turns the key, a thick, sweet-smelling humidity drafts up from the dark cistern stairs, clinging to his skin.
