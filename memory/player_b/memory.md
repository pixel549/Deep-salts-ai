# Character B's private memory

Only what this character has personally witnessed or been told.

[turn 1] I: I adjust the heavy pack on my shoulders, making sure the weight of the cudgel is balanced against my scale case. 'Not exactly the welcome wagon, is it?' I mutter to the empty air, trying to keep my voice steady despite the damp chill seeping into my bones. I walk toward the massive doors, stopping only briefly to wipe the condensation from the brass-framed directory with the cuff of my glove. I'm looking for the offices or whoever might be left to talk business with, before my time—or the weather—turns for the worse. (I need to get inside and find someone authorized to sell. The silence is unsettling, and the atmosphere feels heavy with more than just sulfur. My granddaughter's health is the only thing that matters, so I need to find the administrative offices to start the negotiation for the refined salt, while keeping my eyes peeled for any sign of the primordial-grade stock my mentor whispered about. I'm anxious, but I have to play the part of a regular trader.)
[turn 1] What happened: Maren's eyes scan the brass-framed directory, but the dampness has crept behind the glass; the paper inside is a water-logged ruin of running ink, completely illegible. Corwin wipes the condensation off the outer frame with his glove, but the metal is heavily pitted with green corrosion, leaving only greasy streaks and no useful directions to the administrative offices. The heavy brass-and-glass entry doors yield with a low, wet sigh. Inside, the Grand Vestibule of Stillwell Hydro is choked with a warm, sulfurous fog that clings to the throat. There is no security guard, no reception clerk, and no sound save for the rhythmic, distant dripping of mineral water onto tiles. Underneath that dripping lies a faint, high-frequency vibration—a hum that resonates uncomfortably in the jaw. The oppressive stillness of this massive, empty entrance hall, where a grand health resort should be bustling, presses heavily against both of their minds.

[turn 2] I: I step toward the reception desk, my movements deliberate to mask the way the hum in the floorboards is making my teeth ache. I give the brass bell a sharp, firm strike. 'Stillwell Hydro!' I call out, pitching my voice to be warm and unmistakably professional, loud enough to travel through the fog. 'Corwin Tarrow, here for a trade consultation. I know it’s a bit quiet today, but I’ve got my credentials ready and a fair offer on the table for the refined stocks.' I lean slightly over the mahogany slab, looking for any sign of a clerk—or something else—behind that dark glass door, while my hand instinctively hovers near the weight at my hip. (The place is eerie, and that vibration is setting off all my alarm bells. I need to make sure I'm following 'protocol' to avoid any immediate trouble, but the main goal is to find an administrator or someone who has access to the salt. The bell is a test; if no one answers, I'll have to choose between pushing into the administration office or heading toward the terraces/changing rooms. I'm keeping my posture relaxed but ready for the unexpected.)
[turn 2] What happened: The brass bell's ring is instantly choked by the wet, heavy air, producing a flat, dead iron clink instead of a chime. Maren pulls the leather guest register toward her, but the paper is bloated and soft with mineral moisture; the ink has run into dark, weeping violet bruises. When she tries to focus on the handwriting, the letters seem to slip and dissolve under her gaze, entirely illegible. Corwin leans over the desk, shouting his greeting into the gloom behind the dark glass door. His eyes strain to pierce the pane, but on his sharp strike of the bell, the dark glass acts as a mirror—showing his own reflection, but horribly distorted: his eyes look like empty, salt-crusted hollows, and a tall, featureless shadow seems to stand directly behind his shoulder. He jerks back as the hum in the floorboards suddenly spikes, vibrating up through his boots and into his jaw like a drill, making his molars ache with a cold, metallic taste. Then, the latch of the dark glass door clicks. It swings open. A thin man in a high, stiff collar and a waxed-canvas coat steps out. His skin is impossibly dry for this humid lobby, and his eyes are wide, glassy, and unblinking. He ignores Corwin's credentials and Maren's hand on the book, calmly sliding the ruined register shut with one pale finger. 'Good afternoon,' the clerk says, his voice flat and perfectly rehearsed. 'Stillwell Hydro is operating under restricted therapeutic protocols. If you are here for a resident, I must inform you they are taking the deep cure and cannot be disturbed.'

[turn 3] I: I offer a polite, practiced smile, letting the professional warmth slide over my face despite the lingering ache in my jaw. I keep my hands visible, resting them on the counter away from my cudgel. 'A deep cure, you say? That's a shame. I suppose that means the administrative offices are similarly restricted.' I gesture vaguely toward the door behind him, my voice steady. 'I'm Corwin Tarrow, a licensed merchant. I'm looking to move some inventory—refined stock specifically—to keep the trade routes steady. If the administration is currently occupied, perhaps you could point me toward the storage foreman or a clerk with authority to sign off on a bulk transfer? I’d hate to leave the floor empty-handed when there’s business to be done.' (This clerk is unnerving, especially that unnatural dryness, but he is the gatekeeper. I need to get into the administration office or find the primordial-grade salt, and this guy is my best lead. Pushing too hard or getting aggressive might get me kicked out before I can even ask for what I need. I'll act the part of the boring, persistent trader—if I can convince him I'm just here for the standard, boring refined salt, he might drop his guard enough for me to maneuver toward the storage areas.)
[turn 3] What happened: Maren's formal demands slide off the clerk like water off waxed canvas. He does not so much as blink at her leather-bound logbook, his gaze remaining fixed on the empty air slightly above her shoulder. 'All administrative and personnel logs are currently undergoing active sanitization,' he intones, his voice a flat, dead drone. 'The records are sealed until the cycle completes.' However, when Corwin steps in with his easy merchant's smile and inquiries about bulk transfers, the clerk's neck hinges sideways with a dry, clicking sound. 'Refined stock,' the clerk repeats. 'The bath-salts are drawn and stored below. The storage foreman, Mr. Vance, manages the lower cisterns. He has not returned to his post.' Slowly, the clerk reaches beneath the polished brass counter and produces a heavy, cold brass key, sliding it across the wood toward Corwin. 'You may seek him in the service corridors. Return the key when your business is concluded.' The heavy iron gate leading down to the Lower Corridor stands unlocked, though the dark, humid administrative office remains locked and silent beside them.
